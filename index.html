      AI money playbooks

      Practical finance workflows you can use today.

      Each playbook solves one specific money problem. Copy the AI prompt, open ChatGPT or Claude, and follow the steps.

      Budget

      Build a weekly budget in 10 minutes

      Who: Anyone who has avoided budgeting because it felt complicated

      Solves: Not knowing where money goes week to week

      You get: A simple weekly split + recurring Sunday review habit

      Copy this AI prompt

      I want to build a simple weekly budget. My monthly take-home income is [AMOUNT]. My fixed expenses include [LIST]. My flexible spending usually goes toward [CATEGORIES]. Create a realistic weekly budget that splits income into needs, wants, savings, and a small buffer. Keep it simple enough to actually maintain.

      See full workflow + recommended apps →
      Spending

      Turn messy spending into categories

      Who: Anyone with confusing bank statements or random charges

      Solves: Not knowing what you actually spent money on

      You get: Clear categories, totals, and your biggest surprise

      Copy this AI prompt

      Here are my anonymized transactions for the month: [PASTE MERCHANT NAME + AMOUNT, one per line, no account numbers or personal info]. Group these into spending categories, calculate the total per category, identify my top 3, and flag any recurring charges. Give me a short summary and one realistic suggestion.

      See full workflow + recommended apps →
      Debt

      Create a debt payoff plan

      Who: Anyone juggling multiple debts with no clear order

      Solves: Paying minimums forever without a path to freedom

      You get: Snowball vs. avalanche comparison with timelines

      Copy this AI prompt

      Help me create a debt payoff plan. My debts are: [LIST EACH: Name, Balance, Interest Rate %, Minimum Monthly Payment]. Show me the payoff order and estimated timeline using both the snowball method and avalanche method. Which saves more in total interest? Use plain numbers.

      Investing

      Build a beginner investing learning path

      Who: Anyone curious about investing but not sure where to start

      Solves: Feeling overwhelmed by investing information

      You get: A personalized 8-week education plan with weekly actions

      Copy this AI prompt

      I am a beginner investor. My knowledge level is [BEGINNER / SOME BASICS / SOME EXPERIENCE]. I can invest about $[AMOUNT] per month and my time horizon is [SHORT / MEDIUM / LONG]. Create a personalized 8-week investing education plan. Each week: one concept to learn, one question to answer, one action to take. Focus on index funds and long-term strategy. Add a disclaimer.

      Career

      Write a networking email for finance internships

      Who: Finance students reaching out to analysts, associates, or recruiters

      Solves: Sending generic emails that get ignored

      You get: Two personalized email drafts ready to send

      Copy this AI prompt

      Help me write a cold networking email for a finance internship. My name is [NAME], a [YEAR/MAJOR] at [SCHOOL]. I am reaching out to [THEIR NAME], a [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. I am interested in [TEAM/ROLE]. One real thing I know about this company is [FACT]. Write a subject line and 3-sentence email with a low-pressure ask. Give me two versions: formal and slightly warmer.

      Research

      Compare financial tools before choosing one

      Who: Anyone evaluating budgeting apps, brokerages, or credit cards

      Solves: Choosing tools based on marketing instead of real needs

      You get: A structured comparison with pros, cons, and costs

      Copy this AI prompt

      Compare these financial tools: [LIST TOOL NAMES]. I am trying to [GOAL]. For each tool: what it does, who it is best for, monthly cost, main downside, and where to find trustworthy reviews. Do not tell me which to pick — give me the information to decide. Keep each to 4–5 sentences.
